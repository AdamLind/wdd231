How It Started

Frustrated by the lack of accessible, centralized data on outages, and driven by a passion for clean energy and data visualization, the founder (Adam Lind) set out to build a platform that made this critical information both easy to understand and freely available.

How It's Going

We’re committed to keeping the site fast, accurate, and user-friendly. From integrating new data sources to rolling out backend upgrades, our focus remains on transparency, reliability, and usability. Whether it's a minor UI tweak or a major system overhaul, we're constantly building toward a smarter way to understand nuclear energy.

Our Values

At the heart of Nuclear Outage Tracker is a shared belief in the power of transparency, curiosity, and responsible innovation. The engineers behind the site are driven by a few simple but important ideals:

  • Clarity Through Data – We believe complex systems like nuclear energy should be understood, not mystified. By turning raw data into clear visuals, we aim to make nuclear information accessible to everyone.
  • Curiosity-Driven Work – This project is fueled by a genuine passion for science, energy, and problem-solving. We ask questions, explore possibilities, and build tools that we ourselves want to use.
  • Open Knowledge – We support the idea that information—especially about critical infrastructure—should be open, accurate, and easy to find. Knowledge empowers better decisions.
  • Constant Improvement – Whether it's a tiny bug fix or a major new feature, we're committed to steady, thoughtful progress. We don’t believe in perfection, just iteration.
